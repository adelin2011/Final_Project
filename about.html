Anyone Can Cook

When Chef Auguste Gusteau founded the restaurant, he meant for his dishes to reflect his famed cookbook, "Anyone Can Cook." After his death and years of restaurant obsurity, Gusteau's son, Linguini, came to work in the kitchen, renewing the public's interest.

Linguini lived up to the his father's former success with the help of a friend (Remy, a small mouse helping in the kitchen). Remy and Linguini have made sure that Gusteau's is the top restaurant in Paris.
